## What you'll find here

Each blog post tells the story of building one learning lab. I follow a pretty consistent pattern:

- Opening: Why I got excited about the topic (usually after being confused)
- What I built: Brief overview of the lab's 6 stations
- Biggest challenge: The specific disaster I created (with real numbers!)
- What Prof. Teeters taught me: The lesson that solved my problem
- Try it out: Link to the actual lab so you can practice

I don't skip the messy parts. If I broke something, you'll hear about it. If I misunderstood a concept for weeks, I'll tell you. That's the whole point â€” showing the real learning process, not just the polished final result.
